History of College Athletics


Taught by Professor John Bacon
Mon-Wed 8:30-10:00 Fall 2019

This course will explain how football became a public spectacle at the University of Michigan and other campuses. In the process, it will develop the central theme that the marriage between university academics and big-time athletics was not inevitable, but it has thrived anyway, due to the efforts of a few fascinating personalities at a few crucial junctures.

Topics in this course include:

  • Impact of the Northwest Ordinance
  • American Exceptionalism
  • The Athletics and Academics Marriage
  • The Bacon Grand Unified Theory of College Athletics
  • The History of Boxencuben "The Big Brother of Basketball"
